    What is Canva Bulk Create?
    The fastest way to make a full matching card set

    Canva Bulk Create lets you design one card template once, then automatically generate hundreds of cards from a spreadsheet. Each card gets its own vocabulary term, definition, and Spanish translation already filled in. Without Bulk Create, making 20 study cards means duplicating a design 20 times and typing each term by hand.

    Sign up at canva.com/education - completely free for K-12 teachers. Unlocks Bulk Create and all Pro features including AI image generation.

    Understanding your CSV columns
    What is filled in automatically vs what you complete yourself

    When you download the CSV from the builder, open it in Excel or Google Sheets. Each row is one card. Here is what every column contains:

    ColumnWhat it containsStatus
    card_typestudy_card, question_card, or answer_card - use to filter in CanvaAuto-filled
    term_enThe English vocabulary term - maps to {{term_en}} in your templateAuto-filled
    term_esSpanish medical translation - YOU fill this in using Google Translate or a medical dictionaryYou fill in
    definitionGrade-level definition of the term - YOU write or paste this inYou fill in
    questionExam-style question for Q&A pairs - YOU write this in (e.g. What is the longest bone?)You fill in
    subjectBody system label - maps to {{subject}} in your templateAuto-filled
    courseCourse name - maps to {{course}} in your templateAuto-filled
    3
    Completing definitions in Excel or Google Sheets
    The most important step before uploading to Canva

    After downloading your CSV, open it in Google Sheets or Excel. You will see your terms already listed with placeholder text in the definition and Spanish columns. Your job is to fill those in before uploading to Canva.

    1
    Open the CSV in Google Sheets
    Go to sheets.google.com, click New, then File - Import - Upload and select your downloaded CSV file. Choose comma as the separator. Your terms will appear in rows with empty definition and term_es columns.
    2
    Fill in definitions
    Click the definition cell for the first term and type or paste a grade-appropriate definition. For Health Science I, aim for one clear sentence. For A&P, one to two sentences. For Nurse Aide, keep it clinical and concise. Work down the column one row at a time.
    💡 Tip: Use your textbook glossary, Merriam-Webster Medical Dictionary, or MedlinePlus for reliable definitions. Copy and edit them to match your students' reading level.
    3
    Fill in Spanish translations (bilingual mode)
    In the term_es column, type the Spanish medical translation for each term. Google Translate works well for most medical terms - type the English term and switch to Spanish. For terms where accuracy matters most (like medication names or procedures), verify with a medical Spanish reference.
    ⚠️ Always have a bilingual colleague or your ESL teacher review Spanish translations before printing a full class set.
    4
    Save as CSV before uploading to Canva
    Once definitions and Spanish terms are filled in, go to File - Download - Comma Separated Values (.csv). Save this completed file. This is the file you will upload to Canva Bulk Create - not the original downloaded file.
    🎉
    Time-saving tip for large sets
    Use one set of definitions across multiple classes or years. Once you fill in the Skeletal System definitions the first time, save that completed CSV and reuse it next year - just update Canva Bulk Create with the same file.
    4
    Build your Canva template
    Design it once - Bulk Create fills in the rest

    Your template is the master design that every generated card follows. You only build this once per card style. The key is adding text placeholders - special text boxes with column names in double curly braces that Canva replaces with your real data.

    1
    Create a new design in Canva
    Go to canva.com, click Create a design, then Custom size. For a standard 5 x 3 inch flashcard enter 5 inches wide by 3 inches tall. Click Create new design.
    2
    Design your card front layout
    Add text boxes, shapes, and image frames. Style everything - font, size, color, spacing - exactly how you want all cards to look. This is the only time you need to worry about design.
    3
    Add text placeholders - the critical step
    Click any text box that should pull data from your CSV. Type the column name in double curly braces exactly as shown: {{term_en}} for the vocabulary term, {{term_es}} for Spanish, {{definition}} for the definition, {{subject}} for the body system label. Spelling must match your CSV column headers exactly - lowercase with underscores, no spaces inside the braces.
    ⚠️ Common mistake: typing {{Term_EN}} or {{ term_en }} with wrong capitalization or extra spaces. Canva is case-sensitive.
    4
    Add a second page for the card back
    Click the plus button below your first page to add page 2. Design the card back - add a large image frame for the illustration, or add {{definition}} if you want the definition on the back instead of the front.
    5
    Name and save your template
    Canva saves automatically. Give your design a clear name like Health Science Flashcard Template - Skeletal System. You can reuse it for other body systems with a new CSV file - no redesign needed.
    5
    Run Bulk Create and generate cards
    From completed CSV to a full card set in under a minute
    1
    Open your template in Canva
    Go to your Canva Home page, find your flashcard template, and click to open it in the editor.
    2
    Open Bulk Create from the Apps panel
    In the left sidebar, click Apps - then search for Bulk Create and click to open it. If you do not see it, make sure you are on a Canva for Education or Pro account.
    3
    Upload your completed CSV
    In Bulk Create, click Upload data then Upload CSV. Select the completed CSV file (the one where you filled in definitions and Spanish translations in Google Sheets). Canva shows a preview of the columns - verify they look correct.
    4
    Connect columns to placeholders and generate
    Canva tries to auto-match your CSV columns to the placeholders in your template. Check that each placeholder is connected to the correct column. Click Continue, then Generate designs. Canva builds one card per row in about 10-30 seconds.

    What used to take 45 minutes of manual card creation now takes under 5 minutes once your CSV is complete.
    6
    Q&A pairs walkthrough
    Creating physical matching cards for stations and partner work

    Q&A pairs produce two cards per term - a question card (showing an exam question) and an answer card (showing the term). Students spread both sets on a desk and physically match them. This is excellent for stations, partner review, and sorting activities.

    📋
    Q&A pairs require two templates and two CSV uploads
    Create two Canva templates - one for question cards with {{question}} as the main text, one for answer cards with {{term_en}}. Run Bulk Create twice: once filtered to question_card rows, once to answer_card rows. Filter by the card_type column in Google Sheets.
    1
    Fill in the question column in Google Sheets
    Open your CSV in Google Sheets. In the question column, write an exam-style question for each term - for example, What is the longest bone in the human body? or Which structure produces red blood cells? Keep questions concise so they fit on a card.
    2
    Filter to question_card rows and save a separate file
    In Google Sheets, click the card_type column header, go to Data - Create a filter, then filter to show only question_card rows. Select all visible rows, copy, and paste into a new sheet. Download that as a CSV named something like skeletal-questions.csv.
    3
    Run Bulk Create for question cards, then answer cards
    Build a question card template with {{question}} as the main text and {{subject}} as a label. Run Bulk Create with the questions CSV. Then filter to answer_card rows, build an answer card template with {{term_en}} and {{term_es}}, and run Bulk Create again.
    7
    Printing and sharing
    1
    Export as PDF for printing
    Click Share - Download - PDF Print for the highest quality. For double-sided cards, use flip on short edge for landscape cards, or flip on long edge for portrait cards. Always print one test sheet before the full set. Print on 65lb card stock or heavier for durability.
    2
    Share digitally with students
    Click Share - Share link - Anyone with the link can view. Students open the link in any browser without needing a Canva account. You can also share the PDF through Google Classroom or your school LMS.
    3
    Reuse your template next unit
    Your Canva template stays saved. Next unit, come back to the CSV Builder, select the new body system, download a new CSV, fill in definitions, and run Bulk Create on the same template. The whole process takes about 10 minutes once you have done it once.

    Troubleshooting and FAQ
    My placeholders show as {{term_en}} in the final cards and are not being replaced
    Almost always a spelling or format mismatch. Check that your CSV column header is spelled exactly like your placeholder - term_en not Term_EN. Use double curly braces - {{term_en}} not {term_en}. No spaces inside the braces. Canva is case-sensitive.
    Bulk Create is not showing up in my Apps panel
    Bulk Create requires Canva for Education, Pro, or Teams. Sign up for Canva for Education at canva.com/education - it is completely free for verified K-12 teachers.
    How do I open the CSV file in Google Sheets?
    Go to sheets.google.com and click the plus button to create a new sheet. Then go to File - Import - Upload, and drag in your downloaded CSV file. When prompted, choose Comma as the separator type and click Import data. Your terms will appear in rows ready to edit.
    How do I filter the CSV to just question_card or answer_card rows in Google Sheets?
    Click the card_type column header. Go to Data - Create a filter. Click the filter dropdown that appears in the column header. Uncheck everything except the row type you want. Your view now shows only those rows. Select all visible rows, copy, paste into a new sheet, and download as CSV.
    The definition text overflows the text box on the card
    Turn on Auto-resize text on the definition text box in Canva - click the text box, then Resize and position, then turn on Auto-fit. Canva will shrink the font automatically. Alternatively, keep definitions to one sentence for Health Science I or use a larger card size like 4 x 6 inches.
    My double-sided cards are not aligning when I print
    Make sure both pages of your Canva design are exactly the same size. In your printer settings try switching between flip on short edge and flip on long edge to see which aligns correctly for your card orientation. Always print one test sheet first. Some teachers prefer printing fronts and backs separately and gluing them back-to-back to avoid alignment issues entirely.
    Can I share my Canva template with other teachers in my department?
    Yes. In Canva click Share - Copy link - Can use as template. Other teachers with the link can open the template in their own Canva account and run their own Bulk Create sessions with their own CSV files. Each teacher needs their own Canva for Education account.
