    What is Canva Bulk Create?
    The fastest way to make a full matching card set

    Canva Bulk Create lets you design one card template once, then automatically generate hundreds of cards from a spreadsheet — each with its own vocabulary term, definition, Spanish translation, and image prompt already filled in. Without it, making 20 study cards means duplicating a design 20 times and typing each term by hand.

    🎓
    Canva for Education is free for teachers
    Sign up at canva.com/education — completely free for K–12 teachers. Unlocks Bulk Create, AI image generation, and all Pro features.
    2
    Understanding your CSV file
    What each column contains and how it's used

    The CSV you download is a spreadsheet where each row = one card. Open it in Excel or Google Sheets to preview it before uploading to Canva.

    ColumnWhat it containsUsed for
    card_typestudy_card, question_card, or answer_cardFiltering
    term_enEnglish vocabulary term (e.g., Femur)Card front
    term_esSpanish medical translation — bilingual mode onlyCard front
    definitionGrade-level definition of the termCard front
    questionExam-style question — Q&A mode onlyQuestion card
    image_promptPaste into Canva AI image generator for front illustrationCard front
    best_viewAnatomical view used (Anterior, Cross-section, etc.)Reference
    back_image_promptDescription for the card back illustrationCard back
    subjectBody system label (e.g., Skeletal System)Card front
    courseCourse name (e.g., Health Science I)Card front
    3
    Build your Canva template
    Design it once — Bulk Create fills in the rest

    Your template is the master design every card follows. The key is adding text placeholders — text boxes with column names in double curly braces that Canva replaces with your real data.

    1
    Create a new design in Canva
    Go to canva.com → Create a design → Custom size. For a 5×3 inch flashcard, enter 5 inches wide × 3 inches tall. Click Create new design.
    2
    Design your card front layout
    Add text boxes, shapes, and image frames. Style everything — font, size, color, spacing — exactly how you want all cards to look. This is the only time you need to worry about design.
    3
    Add text placeholders — the critical step
    Click any text box that should pull CSV data. Type the column name in double curly braces: {{term_en}} {{term_es}} {{definition}} {{subject}}. Spelling must match your CSV column headers exactly — lowercase, underscores, no spaces inside braces.
    ⚠️ Common mistake: {{Term_EN}} or {{ term_en }} (wrong capitalization or spaces). Canva is case-sensitive.
    4
    Add page 2 for the card back
    Click + below your first page to add page 2. Design the card back — add a large image frame for the illustration. If using image + term, add {{term_en}} at the bottom.
    5
    Save your template
    Canva saves automatically. Name it clearly — e.g., "Health Science Flashcard Template — Skeletal System." Reuse it for other body systems with a new CSV.
    4
    Study cards: full walkthrough
    1
    Open your template in Canva
    Go to your Home page → find your flashcard template → click to open it in the editor.
    2
    Open Bulk Create from the Apps panel
    In the left sidebar, click Apps → search for Bulk Create → click to open it.
    🔍 Can't find it? Try searching "bulk" in the Apps search bar. Requires Canva for Education or Pro.
    3
    Upload your CSV file
    In Bulk Create → Upload data → Upload CSV → select your downloaded file. Canva shows a preview of the columns. Verify they appear correctly.
    4
    Connect columns to placeholders and generate
    Canva tries to auto-match columns to placeholders. Check each connection. Click Continue → Generate designs. For 20 terms you get 20 two-page card designs in about 10–30 seconds.
    🎉
    You just saved hours of work
    What used to take 45+ minutes of manual card creation now takes under 5 minutes from start to a complete generated set.
    5
    Adding AI illustrations
    Using the image_prompt column with Canva AI Image
    ⚠️
    Bulk Create does not auto-generate images
    Canva's Bulk Create only fills text placeholders — not images. You'll add AI illustrations card by card after generating. For 15–20 cards this takes about 10–15 minutes.
    1
    Find the image prompt for each card
    Keep your CSV open in Excel or Google Sheets. Find the row for the term on the card you're working on. Copy the text in the image_prompt column.
    2
    Click the image placeholder in Canva → Generate image
    Click the empty image frame. In the toolbar, look for AI image or Generate image. Click it, paste your image_prompt text, and click Generate. Choose the most accurate result.
    3
    Add the card back illustration
    Switch to page 2 of that card. Click the image placeholder, open AI image, paste the back_image_prompt from the same row. Repeat for each card.
    6
    Q&A pairs: full walkthrough
    Creating a physical matching card set for stations and partner work

    Q&A pairs produce two cards per term — a question card (showing an exam question) and an answer card (showing the term and image). Students spread both sets on a desk and physically match them.

    📋
    Q&A pairs require two templates and two CSV uploads
    Create two Canva templates — one for question cards, one for answer cards. Run Bulk Create twice: once filtered to question_card rows, once to answer_card rows. Use the card_type column to filter.
    1
    Filter your CSV for question cards
    Open your CSV in Google Sheets or Excel. Filter the card_type column to show only question_card rows. Save as a separate file (e.g., skeletal-questions.csv).
    2
    Build a question card template with {{question}}
    Create a Canva design with {{question}} as the main text in large, readable font, and {{subject}} as a smaller label. Use a distinctive color so question cards look different from answer cards.
    3
    Run Bulk Create → repeat for answer cards
    Open your question template → Apps → Bulk Create → upload the filtered question CSV → connect columns → Generate. Then filter the original CSV to answer_card rows, build an answer card template with {{term_en}} and an image frame, and repeat.
    7
    Printing & sharing
    1
    Export as PDF for printing
    Click Share → Download → PDF Print. For double-sided cards, use flip on short edge for landscape cards, or flip on long edge for portrait. Always print one test sheet first before running the full set.
    🖨 Print on 65lb card stock or heavier for durability.
    2
    Share digitally
    Click Share → Share link → Anyone with the link can view. Students open in any browser — no Canva account needed. You can also share the PDF through Google Classroom or your school's LMS.
    3
    Reuse your template next unit
    Your Canva template stays saved. Next unit, run the CSV builder for the new body system, download a fresh CSV, and run Bulk Create on the same template. Takes about 5 minutes once you've done it once.
    8
    Troubleshooting & FAQ
    My placeholders show as {{term_en}} in the final cards — they're not being replaced
    Almost always a spelling or format mismatch. Check: (1) CSV column header spelled exactly like your placeholder — term_en not Term_EN. (2) Double curly braces — {{term_en}} not {term_en}. (3) No spaces inside braces — {{ term_en }} will not work. Canva is case-sensitive.
    Bulk Create isn't showing up in my Apps panel
    Bulk Create requires Canva for Education, Pro, or Teams. Sign up for Canva for Education at canva.com/education — it's completely free for verified K–12 teachers.
    The AI-generated illustrations don't look anatomically accurate
    Try generating 2–3 times — results vary. Add style words at the end: "medical textbook illustration, clean white background." For specific structures, a more detailed prompt with additional anatomical terms produces better results.
    How do I filter my CSV to just question_card or answer_card rows?
    In Google Sheets: Click the card_type column header → Data → Create a filter → click the filter dropdown → uncheck everything except the type you want. Select all visible rows → copy → paste into a new Sheet → download as CSV. Same process in Excel with Data → Filter.
    The definition text overflows the text box
    Options: (1) Turn on Auto-resize text on the definition text box (click text box → Resize & position → Auto-fit). (2) Edit shorter definitions directly in your CSV. (3) Use a larger card size like 4×6 inches.
    My double-sided cards aren't aligning when I print
    Make sure both pages are exactly the same size. Try switching between flip on short edge and flip on long edge in your printer settings. Always print one test sheet first. Some teachers prefer printing fronts and backs separately and gluing them back-to-back.
    Can I share the template with other teachers in my department?
    Yes — in Canva, click Share → Copy link → Can use as template. Other teachers with the link can open the template in their own Canva account and run their own Bulk Create sessions with their own CSV files.

    Quick reference: key terms

    Bulk Create
    Canva feature that generates multiple card designs from a CSV spreadsheet automatically
    CSV
    Comma-Separated Values — a plain spreadsheet file that Canva reads to fill in your card data
    Placeholder
    A tag like {{term_en}} in your template that gets replaced with real data during Bulk Create
    image_prompt
    The anatomical illustration description — paste into Canva's AI image generator for each card
    card_type
    Column identifying a row as study_card, question_card, or answer_card — used to filter your CSV
    Canva for Education
    Free professional Canva account for verified K–12 teachers — includes Bulk Create and AI image generation
